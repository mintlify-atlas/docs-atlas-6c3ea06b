---
title: "Disputes"
description: "Raise and manage disputes for transactions you believe are incorrect or unauthorized."
---

The Disputes module lets you formally challenge a transaction you believe is erroneous, fraudulent, or otherwise problematic. Both parties to the original transaction can participate in the dispute discussion, and an administrator mediates the resolution.

## When to raise a dispute

Raise a dispute if:

- You sent money and the goods or services were not received.
- An unauthorized transfer appeared on your account.
- You received an incorrect amount.
- A merchant payment was processed but the order was not fulfilled.

<Warning>
Disputes should only be raised for genuine issues. Misuse of the dispute process may result in account restrictions.
</Warning>

## Opening a dispute

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the transaction">
    Go to your transaction list or dashboard and locate the transaction you want to dispute.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Raise Dispute">
    On the transaction detail page, click the **Raise Dispute** button. This opens the dispute creation form at `/dispute/add/{transaction_id}`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the dispute form">
    Complete all required fields:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Title | Short summary of the issue |
    | Reason | Select from the pre-configured dispute reasons |
    | Description | Detailed explanation of the problem |

    The `claimant_id` (you) and `defendant_id` (other party) and `transaction_id` are set automatically from the transaction context.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the dispute">
    Click **Submit**. A dispute record is created with `status = 'Open'` and a unique code in the format `DIS-XXXXXX`.

    Email notifications are sent to:
    - The **defendant** (the other party in the transaction)
    - The **platform administrator**

    via `OpenDisputeMailService`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Only one dispute can be raised per transaction. If a dispute already exists for the transaction, you will see the error: *"A dispute already exists for this payment."*
</Note>

## Dispute statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Open` | Dispute is active; discussion is ongoing |
| `Resolved` | Dispute has been closed with a decision |
| `Closed` | Dispute ended without further action |

## Participating in the dispute discussion

Both the claimant and the defendant can add messages and attach evidence files to the dispute thread.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the dispute">
    From **Disputes** (`/disputes`), click on a dispute to open the discussion view.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a reply">
    Type your message in the reply box. You can also attach a supporting file (image or document). Accepted file types follow the platform's configured file extension rules.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the reply">
    Click **Send**. Your reply is saved as a `DisputeDiscussion` entry with `type = 'User'`. The other party receives an email notification via `DisputeReplyMailService`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Discussion is only possible while the dispute has `status = 'Open'`. If the dispute is resolved or closed, the message *"Dispute discussion already ended."* is shown.
</Note>

## Dispute evidence

You can upload supporting files to strengthen your case:

- Receipts, screenshots, or photos
- Files are stored in `uploads/files/`
- Download links are provided to all parties and the admin in the discussion thread

## Administrator resolution

The platform administrator reviews the dispute thread, all uploaded evidence, and any messages from both parties. The admin can:

- Update the dispute `status` to `Resolved` or `Closed`.
- Declare a `winner_id` if the dispute warrants a financial decision.
- Manually adjust wallet balances if required.

## Viewing your disputes

All disputes where you are the claimant or defendant are listed at `/disputes`. You can filter by:

- **Status**: Open, Resolved, Closed, or All
- **Date range**: from/to date filters

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="How long does dispute resolution take?">
Resolution time depends on the complexity of the case and administrator availability. Keep the discussion thread updated with relevant information to speed up the process.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I close a dispute I opened?">
Currently only administrators can change the dispute status. If you wish to withdraw a dispute, contact support with your dispute code (e.g. `DIS-XXXXXX`).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens after the dispute is resolved?">
If a financial adjustment is warranted, the administrator makes the necessary wallet balance corrections manually. You will receive an email notification with the outcome.
</Accordion>
