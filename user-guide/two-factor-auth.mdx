---
title: "Two-Factor Authentication"
description: "Secure your account with SMS, email, or Google Authenticator two-step verification."
---

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security beyond your password. DossPanel supports three verification methods: SMS (phone), email, and Google Authenticator (TOTP).

## Available 2FA methods

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="SMS / Phone" icon="mobile">
    A 6-digit code is sent to your registered phone number via the configured SMS gateway.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Email" icon="envelope">
    A 6-digit code is sent to your registered email address.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Google Authenticator" icon="shield">
    A time-based one-time password (TOTP) generated by the Google Authenticator app or any compatible TOTP app.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Enabling two-step verification

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open security settings">
    Go to **Profile → Security** or navigate to `/profile/two-fa`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a verification method">
    Select one of the three available methods: **Phone**, **Email**, or **Google Authenticator**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Receive and enter the verification code">
    - **Phone**: a 6-digit code is sent via SMS to your `formattedPhone`. Enter it in the prompt.
    - **Email**: a 6-digit code is sent to your registered email. Enter it in the prompt.
    - **Google Authenticator**: a QR code is generated inline using the `pragmarx.google2fa` library. Scan it with your authenticator app, then enter the 6-digit TOTP to confirm setup.

    Once the code is verified, `two_step_verification` is set to `1` in `user_details` and your chosen method is stored in `two_step_verification_type`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setting up Google Authenticator

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install an authenticator app">
    Download **Google Authenticator** (iOS / Android) or any TOTP-compatible app such as Authy or 1Password.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Google Authenticator in DossPanel">
    On the Two-FA settings page, select **Google Authenticator**. DossPanel generates a secret key and displays a QR code inline.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app, tap the **+** button, and scan the QR code. The account will appear in the app labelled with your email address and the platform name.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the 6-digit TOTP">
    The app displays a rotating 6-digit code. Enter the current code in DossPanel to confirm the pairing. The `google2fa_secret` is saved to your user record and `two_step_verification_type` is set to `google_authenticator`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The TOTP code refreshes every 30 seconds. Enter the code promptly — if it expires before you submit, wait for the next code.
</Note>

## Verifying 2FA on login

After entering your password at login, DossPanel checks `two_step_verification_type`:

- **Phone / Email**: a new 6-digit code is dispatched and you are redirected to the verification screen at `/two-fa/verify`.
- **Google Authenticator**: you are redirected to `/two-fa/google-verify` where you enter the current TOTP from your app.

Once verified, a `2fa` key is stored in the session to mark the login as fully authenticated.

## Remember this device

On the verification screen you can check **Remember this device**. DossPanel stores a browser fingerprint in the `device_logs` table. On subsequent logins from the same browser, the 2FA step is skipped automatically.

## Disabling two-step verification

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open security settings">
    Navigate to `/profile/two-fa`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the 'Off' option">
    Change the verification type to disabled. An AJAX request updates `two_step_verification_type` in `user_details`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Disabling 2FA reduces the security of your account. Only do this if you have another security measure in place or you are on a trusted private device.
</Warning>

## Removing Google Authenticator

To unlink Google Authenticator without disabling 2FA entirely, navigate to **Profile → 2FA** and select a different method (phone or email). Your `google2fa_secret` will be cleared (`null`) from your user record.

Alternatively, if you navigate to `/doss/2fa/delete`, the `google2fa_secret` is set to `null` directly and you are redirected back to the 2FA settings page.

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="I lost access to my authenticator app">
Contact support to initiate the account recovery process. An administrator can reset your `google2fa_secret` after verifying your identity through KYC or another manual process.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I'm not receiving SMS codes">
Make sure your phone number is verified in your profile. Check that the SMS gateway is active and that your carrier is not blocking messages from short codes.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I use a different TOTP app?">
Yes. Any app that supports the TOTP (RFC 6238) standard, such as Authy, Microsoft Authenticator, or 1Password, can be used to scan the QR code.
</Accordion>
