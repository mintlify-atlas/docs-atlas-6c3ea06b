---

The Merchant module enables you to create a business profile on DossPanel, accept payments from customers, and view detailed payment records. Merchants can also be organized into groups for administrative purposes.

## Becoming a merchant

You can register as a merchant either during initial account creation (by selecting the Merchant account type) or by creating a merchant profile from within an existing user account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Merchant">
    Navigate to **Merchant** in the main menu or go to `/merchant`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add New Merchant">
    Click the **Add** button to open the merchant creation form (`/merchant/add`).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in merchant details">
    Complete all required fields:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Business Name | Unique name for your store (validated for uniqueness) |
    | Site URL | Your website or payment page URL (must be a valid URL) |
    | Type | Merchant type as configured by the admin |
    | Note | Description of your business |
    | Address | Physical address of the business |
    | Logo | Optional image file (`jpg`, `jpeg`, `png`, `gif`, `bmp`) |
    | Currency | Select from active fiat currencies |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the merchant">
    Submit the form. A QR code may be generated for your merchant profile automatically. The merchant appears in your merchant list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Merchant payment page

Each merchant has a unique payment URL that customers can visit to pay you. The payment page shows your business name, logo, accepted currency, and a payment form.

Customers can pay using the configured merchant payment methods (e.g. wallet-to-wallet transfer within DossPanel).

## Viewing merchant transactions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open merchant payments">
    From your merchant list, click **Payments** next to a merchant to see all transactions associated with that store.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Filter and search">
    Transactions can be searched and filtered by date range, status, and other criteria.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export transactions">
    Use the **Export** button to download merchant payments as an Excel file via `UserMerchantPaymentsExport`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Merchant cashiers

Merchants can create **Cashier** accounts — sub-users who can accept payments on behalf of the merchant. Cashiers have their own login, a dedicated QR code (`object_type = 'cashier'`), and access only to the cashier dashboard.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Merchant → Cashiers">
    From your merchant settings, navigate to the Cashiers section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a cashier">
    Provide the cashier's name, email, and password. The cashier account is created with `role_id` set to the cashier role and linked to your merchant (`doss_id`).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Cashier accepting payments">
    Cashiers log in and scan customer QR codes or share their own QR code to receive payments. Payments made through a cashier appear in the merchant's transaction history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Merchant groups

Administrators can group merchants together using `MerchantGroup`. Groups allow batch reporting or applying specific rules to a set of merchants. As a merchant user you can view which group your merchant belongs to from the merchant detail page.

## Exporting cashier and shift data

Merchants with cashier operations can export:

- **Cashier list**: all cashier accounts linked to the merchant via `UserCasheirsExport`.
- **Cashier payments**: individual cashier transaction history via `EachCashierPaymentsExport`.
- **Cashier shifts**: shift start/end records via `CashierShiftsExport`.

<Tip>
Keep your merchant business name unique and descriptive — it is what customers see on the payment page and in their transaction receipts.
</Tip>
